Marine Life Impact

Microplastics in the
Food Chain

Plastic breaks down into tiny particles called microplastics, which are eaten by small marine organisms. These particles move up the food chain and can eventually affect larger animals and even humans.

Ocean Ecosystem Damage

Plastic pollution disrupts the balance of ocean ecosystems by harming habitats, interfering with food chains, and reducing biodiversity. This makes marine environments less stable and less able to support sea life.

Harm to Marine Animals

Marine animals are directly affected when they eat plastic or become trapped in waste. This can cause injuries, internal blockages, starvation, and often leads to death.
